> A boy can never grow up, their toys only getting more and more expensive.

Yes, you heard that right. I recently embarked on a journey to build my very first Gunpla (Gundam Plastic Model), and let me tell you, it was an experience filled with excitement, challenges, and a lot of learning.

## The Beginning

Everybody has some kind of talent. Some can sing, some can dance, some can create things out of nothing. Me? I have skills, I can craft sheets of papers into living creatures (not my design though). I like doing crafts, it gives me chill and teaches me patience, and I got to show off my products to my friends and family.

After some paper crafting, I noticed Gunpla though the internet. I was fascinated by the intricate designs and the level of detail that goes into each model. They can do a lot of cool looking poses too!

## The Build

Choosing a model kit was hard, especially it the first model you are going to build. There are tons of options out there, from simple beginner kits to complex perfect grade models.

There are 6 types of Gunpla grades:

- **Entry Grade (EG)**: ~~Child toys~~ Simple and easy to build, perfect for beginners.
- **High Grade (HG)**: More detailed and articulated, suitable for those with some, but limited, experience.
- **Super Deformed (SD)**: Cute and stylized versions of Gundam models, often with exaggerated proportions.
- **Real Grade (RG)**: Highly detailed with advanced articulation, great for intermediate builders.
- **Master Grade (MG)**: I call it bigger RG, more complex with more parts and details.
- **Perfect Grade (PG)**: The pinnacle of Gunpla, with incredible detail and complexity, meant for experienced builders.
