> A boy can never grow up, their toys only getting more and more expensive.

Yes, you heard that right. I recently embarked on a journey to build my very first Gunpla (Gundam Plastic Model), and let me tell you, it was an experience filled with excitement, challenges, and a lot of learning.

## The Beginning

Everybody has some kind of talent. Some can sing, some can dance, some can create things out of nothing. Me? I have skills, I can craft sheets of papers into living creatures (not my design though). I like doing crafts, it gives me chill and teaches me patience, and I got to show off my products to my friends and family.

After some paper crafting, I noticed Gunpla though the internet. I was fascinated by the intricate designs and the level of detail that goes into each model. They can do a lot of cool looking poses too!

## The Build

Choosing a model kit was hard, especially it the first model you are going to build. There are tons of options out there, from simple beginner kits to complex perfect grade models.

There are 6 types of Gunpla grades:

- **Entry Grade (EG)**: ~~Child toys~~ Simple and easy to build, perfect for beginners.
- **High Grade (HG)**: More detailed and articulated, suitable for those with some, but limited, experience.
- **Super Deformed (SD)**: Cute and stylized versions of Gundam models, often with exaggerated proportions.
- **Real Grade (RG)**: Highly detailed with advanced articulation, great for intermediate builders.
- **Master Grade (MG)**: I call it bigger RG, more complex with more parts and details.
- **Perfect Grade (PG)**: The pinnacle of Gunpla, with incredible detail and complexity, meant for experienced builders.

With my experience in crafting, I decided to go for a RG model. I picked RG RX-78-2 Ver. 2.0, a classic yet never gets old Gundam design. It is called the "ancestor" Gundam after all. Get in the hobby was quite an investment, especially that I did not know if I would stick to the hobby. I had to buy a set of tools including nippers - twice, because the double-bladed one cuts the part so poorly causing white marks on the parts, a hobby knife, and even panel line ink. It cause me around &10 for the tools and $45 for the model kit.

I watched some tutorials on YouTube, quite a long time before I started buying the kit. So yes, I was prepared, and I know what I was doing. I first started by panel lining the parts before cutting them off the runners. I was so nervous doing that, my hand was shaking like crazy, and the ink almost spilled off the line, haha. But it was a good experience, then I learned to be more calmly doing it later on and it worked. Watching the ink flow through the lines was so satisfying. After some experiments, I also learned to take the right amount of ink needed and where to and where not to panel line, some parts just not meant for panel lining.

After a night to let the ink fully dry, it assemble time baby! It was the weekend so I had a full 2 days to build it. On every building timelapse I've seen, they always took every parts off first, cleaned them up carefully before assembling. But I was so afraid that I could not find the right parts, so I just followed the manual step by step, cutting the right parts off the runners, cleaning it up and assembling it right away. It make the building process less repetitive but also reduce the snap fit experience, I forgot quite a lot of the mechanism behind the suit because of that. But after all, I still got to build it myself, and that is what matters. Watching the model come to life piece by piece was an incredible feeling. And seeing the panel lines make the details pop out? Moah, just perfect.

![body-part](/static/images/how-i-built-my-first-gunpla/IMG_2821.JPEG)

My first Gunpla is not all perfect, I broke 2 pieces during the build. Luckily, they were hidden parts so it did not affect the final look. But yeah, practice makes perfect, I guess.

That's not all, the kit comes with stickers too. My god those stickers are freaking tiny! Some are just like 2 millimeters long, are you kidding me? Placing them stressed me out so much, I had to use the hobby knife tip, and guess what, a toothpick to place them properly. It took me like 8 hours to finish the stickers alone. But again, the Gunpla looks so much better with them on.

## The Result

After hours of building, I finally completed my first Gunpla! The RG RX-78-2 Ver. 2.0 stood proudly on my desk, doing cool poses. Looking at it, I felt a sense of accomplishment, like wow, I actually did that! I guess I got hooked to the hobby now, can't wait to spend more time and money building more Gunpla in the future.

...gotta buy a closet too, they took a lot of space after all. And a posing base, and some sand papers, better tools, camera too,... yeah, it's an expensive hobby.
