> The urban city lifestyle might makes us forget about the true meaning of life without us noticing.

## Insight

I considered myself not an active person, I like to travel, but I'm not gonna be the one to organizes or plans it. And for that reason, I don't travel a lot, I got little experiences about the nature or people in other places, cities or regions.

That was after Tet holiday (Lunar new year) of 2025, my colleague, Ms. An, invited me to join a trip with her to Si Thau Chai. She's got there more than 3 times and said that each time was a memorable experience with her. She talked about how wonderful the atmosphere is, how nice the people are and how each time she came there, her soul finds inner peace,... That made me interested, by the way, she promised that it would be really cheap, just cost less than 2 Million Dongs (around $60 to $80), so I got hooked up and join the trip.

We didn't really make any plan after that, we just booked the bus tickets and the home stay, that's it. As Ms. An said, her plan is to come to the place, walk around until she got tired, and watching people walk by. It's the place and the feeling that matters, not what we do there.

By that time, I was interested in photography. My childhood friend had a Fujifilm XT-20 and he didn't use it during our travel, what a coincidence! I borrowed his camera, learning some basic concepts of photography like ISO, Shutter Speed, Aperture,... and the camera's functionalities. I prepared myself to took some good pictures, and I glad that I did!

## The Ride

We booked the bus at My Dinh station on March 3rd 2025, the trip started at 9pm and took about 5 hours to finish, heading to Lai Chau. The bus has been modified to add "beds" for the customer. It barely lie-able for people who is around 1.8m. The ride was bumpy, I couldn't sleep at all due to the shakiness and the bright head light of the other cars that went the opposite direction. We arrived at 4:30am and took another 15 minutes taxi to get to the top of a mountain, where the village placed.

## Impression

Si Thau Chai welcomed us with a light but freezing rain, it was so early that we barely see anything. We waited under the village gate for about 15 minutes, then the wife of the home stay owner - Mr. A Pao came and guided us to her house near the end of the village. It's a traditional Dao's house, bamboo-made, single-floored with a large guess room in the middle and 3 bedrooms on both sides. By the time we arrived, Mr. A Pao has woke up and has built a fire pit to give us a warm welcome.

![fire-pit](/static/images/traveling-to-si-thau-chai/DSCF1057.JPEG)

Mr. A Pao is a gentle person, he doesn't talk much. He sat there with us, ask if we hungry already, gave us some sweet potatoes to roast it over the fire and prepare our breakfast. Later on, there were 2 cute girls joined us, they also choose Mr. A Pao's house to stay.

![mr-a-pao](/static/images/traveling-to-si-thau-chai/DSCF1061.JPEG)

## Thoughts

The people here live a completely different life. They don't rush for money, they know what is enough. They respect nature and make a living out of it. They care about other people, because they know that sharing love and kindness will bind their village event tighter, and make their community grow even stronger.

Being happy is a choice. You don't need those fancy cars for the latest model shoes to get a sense of satisfaction. You don't have to compare yourself to others, they live a different life, and they do not define your happiness. Just learn to be happy with all you've got, with your own achievements, with your family, your friends,... By doing that, you'll find life much more enjoyable and worth living.
