# The Story Behind

I've been a non-mac user for a long time, using Windows laptops and PCs for years, coding, playing games, movies and everything. But then, at the year of 2023, I got bored, and I got attracted to the Apple ecosystem, and finally decided to give it a try.

First, I bought an Iphone 13, and I kinda liked it, especially the outstanding performance that is not affected by time. So I decided to go further, did a lot of researches and considerations, and bought an used MacBook Pro M1 Pro 14-inch with 16GB RAM in 2025.

Why used M1 Pro in 2025? Because I wanted a powerful machine for development, but I didn't want to spend too much money on the latest models, and I did not sure if I could stick to the ecosystem. The M1 Pro is still a beast in 2025. I bought it for around $1200, which is still a great deal for a powerful, nice looking laptop.

## What I like

- Great performance: The M1 Pro chip is still a powerful beast, handling everything I throw at it, from coding to video editing and more. My productivity has increased significantly since I switched to this machine.
- Excellent battery life: Bought it with 100% battery health - a suspicious number at that time, but it still lasts for around 9 hours of pure coding and web browsing, quite impressive.
- Beautiful display: Yeah, it's a MacBook display, what can I complain?
- Build quality & design: The MacBook Pro is a beautiful piece of hardware, with a sleek design and solid build quality. It feels premium and durable.
- Ecosystem integration: Multiple Apple devices work seamlessly together, making my workflow smoother and more efficient.

## What I don't like

- Aluminum body: Great design, but sometimes I got struck by electricity when touching the laptop, especially in dry weather.
