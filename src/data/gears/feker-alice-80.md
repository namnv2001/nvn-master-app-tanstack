## The Story

I've been a fan of mechanical keyboards for a long time, and I used to use another brand, it's [FL-Esport CMK68 Marshmallow](https://fl-esports.vn/fl-esports-cmk68sam-marshmallow/). The keyboard is gorgeous, I'm in love with the design, the color, the feel, the sound, and everything about it.
But, after a while, 3 years to be exact, I wanted to try something different, maybe a more ergonomic layout...

That's when I found [Feker Alice 80](https://fekertech.com/products/feker-alice) and decided to give it a try. I chose the kit only version and combine with Gateron linear switches (as far as I remember). 
Now, I'm using it as my primary keyboard, and it's great!

## What I Like

- Ergonomic layout, but still easy to learn, gives me a more comfortable typing experience
- The typing sound is a bit dimmed, but it fits the working environment
- The lighting is bright and colorful enough to make it visible even in the daylight (even though I turn it off all the time :v)
- Great battery life, disable lighting and it could last for at least a month or two
- The keyboard supports key mapping using [VIA](https://usevia.app/) website

## What I Don't Like

There's no going back after using VIA, and after that, I can't connect it to my MacBook with the receiver anymore. I have to either use the Bluetooth connection or the USB connection. After some research, I found out that VIA changed the keyboard definition to `USB`, not `KEYBOARD`, therefor, macOS couldn't recognize it as a keyboard and failed to connect to it.
